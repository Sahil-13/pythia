### **Tier 1: Primary Free Resources (No Registration Required)**

#### **1. Project Gutenberg**[^1]

- **URL**: gutenberg.org
- **Content Available**: 75,000+ free eBooks in public domain
- **Mythology Section**: Includes "Myths and Legends of Ancient Greece and Rome" by E.M. Berens, "The Golden Bough" by James George Frazer, "Myths and Legends of China"[^2]
- **Format**: EPUB, Kindle, HTML, TXT
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Quality**: Excellent (professionally proofread)


#### **2. Internet Archive (Archive.org)**[^3][^4]

- **URL**: archive.org
- **Lending Model**: Borrow 10 books simultaneously
- **Loan Duration**: 1-hour browsing or 14-day loans
- **Public Domain Books**: Download unlimited (no DRM)
- **Notable Collections**:
    - "A Short History of Myth" by Karen Armstrong[^5]
    - "Primitive Culture" by Edward Burnett Tylor[^6]
    - "History of Philosophy" by A.C. Grayling[^7]
- **Format**: PDF, EPUB, BookReader (web-based)
- **Cost**: FREE (requires free account for borrowed books)


#### **3. Open Culture (Free Philosophy eBooks)**[^8]

- **URL**: openculture.com
- **Specialization**: 135+ free philosophy eBooks
- **Featured Philosophers**: Aristotle, Hegel, Kant, Nietzsche, Plato, Schopenhauer, Spinoza, Seneca
- **Format**: Kindle, iPad, iPhone, web browser
- **Cost**: FREE


#### **4. Infobooks.org**[^9]

- **URL**: infobooks.org
- **Collections Available**:
    - 50+ Mythology Books
    - 20+ Celtic Mythology Books
    - 15+ Chinese Mythology Books
    - 5+ Egyptian Mythology Books
    - 20+ Roman Mythology Books
- **Format**: PDF download
- **Cost**: FREE (no registration)


#### **5. Internet Classics Archive (MIT)**[^10]

- **URL**: classics.mit.edu
- **Content**: 441 searchable classical works by 59 authors
- **Focus**: Greco-Roman works with some Chinese and Persian texts
- **Format**: Searchable online + downloadable
- **Cost**: FREE

### **Tier 3: Academic Papers \& Research Articles**

#### **1. Academia.edu**[^16]

- **URL**: academia.edu
- **Content**: 55 million research papers
- **Membership**: Join 298 million academics
- **Free Features**:
    - Download papers from researchers who uploaded them
    - Search academic content
    - Follow researchers
- **Premium Features**: Advanced search, PDF packages of 37 related papers, search alerts
- **Cost**: FREE basic (Premium available)


#### **2. ResearchGate**[^17][^18]

- **URL**: researchgate.net
- **Features**: Researchers upload and share their work directly
- **Advantages over traditional publishing**: Faster access, no paywall
- **Interaction**: Ask authors questions, request papers directly
- **Cost**: FREE


#### **3. Philosopher's Index**[^19]

- **URL**: philindex.org
- **Database**: 600,000+ philosophy records from 1902-present
- **Coverage**: All philosophy disciplines, 1,100+ publishers in 148 countries
- **Indexing**: Done by philosophers, includes author-written abstracts (25,000+ subject headings)
- **Limitation**: Some institutional access required, but many university libraries provide free access
- **Cost**: FREE through university libraries

### **Tier 5: Building Your Research Workflow**

**Recommended Workflow for Content Creation:**

1. **Search Phase** (20% of time):
    - Project Gutenberg for classical texts
    - Academia.edu for academic papers
    - Archive.org for borrowable specialty books
    - Vedpuran.net for Indian mythology
2. **Collection Phase** (30% of time):
    - Download and organize PDFs into folders by topic
    - Use citation management: Zotero (free) or Mendeley (freemium)
    - Create a content database with source tracking
3. **Research Phase** (40% of time):
    - Read academic papers via ResearchGate/Academia.edu
    - Cross-reference multiple sources for fact-checking
    - Take timestamped notes with source attribution
4. **Creation Phase** (10% of time):
    - Cite sources in video descriptions
    - Link to original sources for viewers
    - Attribute all images/videos properly
